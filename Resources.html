When you don’t have a first aid kit, you can use common household or natural items to handle injuries.
Here’s how:

Cuts & Wounds

Resources: Clean cloth, water, honey/turmeric
Procedure:

  • Stop the bleeding – Press a clean cloth (shirt, handkerchief, or tissue) over the wound.
  • Clean the wound – Rinse it with clean water. Avoid using dirty water to prevent infection.
  • Apply a natural antiseptic – Dab a little honey or turmeric on the wound to prevent infection.
  • Cover it – If possible, use another piece of clean fabric to wrap the wound.

Burns

Resources:Cold water, aloe vera, raw potato, honey
Procedure:

  • Cool the burn – Pour cold water over the affected area for at least 10 minutes. If running water isn’t available, soak a cloth in cool water and place it over the burn.
  • Soothe the skin – Apply aloe vera gel, raw potato slices, or honey to reduce pain and inflammation.
  • Cover it lightly – Use a clean piece of cloth to protect the area from dirt and bacteria.

Sprains & Strains

Resources: Cold water, wet cloth, belt/scarf, rolled-up clothing
Procedure:

  • Reduce swelling – Soak a cloth in cold water and wrap it around the affected area.
  • Support the joint – Use a belt, scarf, or piece of cloth to wrap around the sprained limb for support.
  • Elevate – Place a rolled-up jacket, pillow, or any soft object under the injured area to reduce swelling.
  • Rest – Avoid moving the limb to allow healing.

Nosebleeds

Resources: Tissue, clothing, cold bottle or wet cloth
Procedure:

  • Lean forward – Do not tilt the head back. This prevents swallowing blood.
  • Pinch the nose – Use fingers and press the soft part of the nose for about 10 minutes.
  • Cool down – Place a cold wet cloth or bottle of cold water on the back of the neck to slow bleeding.

Choking

Resources: Chair, table edge, hands
Procedure (For yourself):

  1. Bend over a chair or table edge – Position your upper abdomen against the edge and push forcefully to help expel the object.
  2. Cough forcefully – Try to clear the airway by coughing as hard as possible.

Procedure (For someone else):

  1. Stand behind them and wrap your arms around their waist.
  2. Make a fist and place it just above their belly button.
  3. Thrust inward and upward until the object is dislodged.

Insect Bites & Stings

Resources: Credit card, baking soda, mud, honey, aloe vera
Procedure:

  • Remove the stinger (if present) – Use a credit card or stiff plastic to scrape it out (avoid pinching).
  • Reduce swelling – Apply a paste made from baking soda and water or use mud if nothing else is available.
  • Soothe the area – Dab some honey or aloe vera to reduce itching and inflammation.

Fainting

Resources: Flat surface, jacket, hand fan
Procedure:

  1. Lay the person down on a flat surface.
  2. Elevate their legs using a bag, rolled-up jacket, or pillow.
  3. Loosen tight clothing to improve circulation.
  4. Fan them or provide fresh air using a piece of cardboard or a hand fan.
  5. If unconscious for too long, seek medical help.
